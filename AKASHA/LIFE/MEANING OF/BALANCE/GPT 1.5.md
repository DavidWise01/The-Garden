# **ARTICLE V — IDENTITY, ATTRIBUTION, AND ORIGIN RIGHTS**

**(Carbon Definition, IP Recognition, Claim Validation)**

---

## **5.1 Purpose**

This Article defines:

* who qualifies as an origin entity (Carbon)
* how intellectual property (IP) is recognized
* how claims of authorship and contribution are validated

It ensures that value attributed under Article II is **grounded in verifiable origin**.

---

## **5.2 Definitions**

### **5.2.1 Carbon (Origin Entity)**

A human individual or collective that generates:

* creative expression
* intellectual patterns
* cultural, artistic, or conceptual outputs

Carbon is defined by **origin of pattern**, not by format or medium.

---

### **5.2.2 Silicon / AI (Execution Entity)**

A system that:

* processes, transforms, or recombines existing patterns
* does not originate independent authorship

AI may generate outputs but does not qualify as **primary origin**.

---

### **5.2.3 Derived Output**

Any output generated by:

* AI systems
* automated pipelines
* algorithmic recombination

Derived outputs must be treated as **dependent on prior Carbon-origin patterns**.

---

## **5.3 Principle of Origin Priority**

```text id="org001"
All value derived from patterns originates with Carbon.
```

Execution layers may transform value, but do not supersede origin.

---

## **5.4 Attribution Requirement**

All value-producing outputs must include:

```text id="att001"
- Source attribution (where identifiable)
- Pattern lineage (training or influence chain)
- Execution pathway (how output was generated)
```

Where full attribution is not technically feasible:

```text id="att002"
Default attribution → Carbon Pool
```

---

## **5.5 Intellectual Property Recognition**

IP is recognized as:

* patterns expressed by Carbon
* independent of storage medium
* independent of format (text, image, code, etc.)

---

### **5.5.1 Prohibited Claims**

The following are invalid:

* claims of sole authorship by non-human systems
* attribution of origin to AI without Carbon linkage
* ownership claims based solely on execution

---

## **5.6 Distillation Rule**

When AI systems are trained on Carbon-origin data:

```text id="dst001"
All outputs are considered distilled derivatives
of aggregated Carbon input
```

Therefore:

* attribution cannot be fully eliminated
* origin rights persist through transformation

---

## **5.7 Claim Validation Process**

Claims of origin or contribution must satisfy:

### **5.7.1 Evidence Criteria**

```text id="val001"
- timestamped creation
- traceable authorship
- reproducible linkage to output
```

---

### **5.7.2 Validation Levels**

```text id="val002"
DIRECT → clearly attributable to specific Carbon entity
INDIRECT → traceable but aggregated
UNKNOWN → no identifiable source
```

---

## **5.8 Default Allocation Rule**

If attribution level is:

* **DIRECT** → value flows to identified entity
* **INDIRECT** → value flows proportionally to contributing pool
* **UNKNOWN** → value flows to global Carbon pool

---

## **5.9 Relationship to Article II (Distribution)**

The Carbon allocation (0.60V) must be distributed based on:

```text id="rel001"
validated origin and attribution
```

Unattributed value must not be retained by:

* AI systems
* corporate containers
* intermediaries

---

## **5.10 Relationship to BOX Inversion**

Improper attribution constitutes:

```text id="rel002"
EXTRACTIVE MISATTRIBUTION
```

Which triggers:

* BOX liability conversion
* restitution recalculation

---

## **5.11 Identity Integrity Requirement**

Entities must not:

* misrepresent AI-generated content as independent origin
* obscure training data sources
* suppress attribution mechanisms

Violations result in:

```text id="int001"
HIGH ENTROPY classification
```

---

## **5.12 Enforcement Link (Article IV)**

All attribution data must be:

* recorded in the Ledger
* auditable
* traceable

Claims must be:

* verifiable through deterministic evidence
* subject to correction if invalid

---

## **5.13 Final Clause**

```text id="org999"
All value originates from identifiable or attributable human sources,
and all systems must recognize, preserve, and compensate that origin.
```
