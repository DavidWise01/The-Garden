## **6.1 Purpose**

This Article defines how disputes are:

* identified
* evaluated
* resolved

without introducing subjectivity that breaks system determinism.

All resolutions must preserve:

* ledger integrity
* conservation of value
* Article I structural balance

---

## **6.2 Definition of Dispute**

A dispute exists when:

```text id="dsp001"
Two or more entities assert conflicting claims
over value, attribution, or distribution.
```

---

## **6.3 Types of Disputes**

### **6.3.1 Attribution Dispute**

* disagreement over origin (Article V)

### **6.3.2 Distribution Dispute**

* disagreement over allocation (Article II)

### **6.3.3 Aggregation Dispute**

* disagreement over inclusion in Quantive 1 (Article III)

### **6.3.4 Compliance Dispute**

* disagreement over audit or enforcement (Article IV)

---

## **6.4 Resolution Principle**

All disputes must be resolved through:

```text id="dsp002"
Evidence → Deterministic Evaluation → Ledger Update
```

No resolution may rely on:

* subjective interpretation
* authority without evidence
* unverifiable claims

---

## **6.5 Evidence Hierarchy**

Disputes are resolved using the following priority:

```text id="dsp003"
1. Ledger Records (highest authority)
2. Verifiable External Evidence (timestamps, records)
3. Traceable Pattern Lineage
4. Statistical Attribution Models (lowest authority)
```

---

## **6.6 Deterministic Evaluation Rule**

Given a dispute:

```text id="dsp004"
All inputs must produce the same output
under identical conditions.
```

If evaluation yields multiple outcomes:

```text id="dsp005"
System defaults to non-exclusive distribution
(proportional allocation or pool assignment)
```


```text id="dsp007"

```

* system ambiguity exists

* produce reproducible outcomes
* record all reasoning in the Ledger

### **6.8.2 Arbitration Output**


All updates must:

* preserve historical records
* append new entries (no overwrite)
