## 1. The three‑body problem as a metaphor  
Three gravitating bodies → **chaos**, no closed‑form solution. Two bodies → stable orbits (ellipses).  
Three political parties → coalition instability, vote splitting, no Condorcet winner. Two parties → stable equilibrium (median voter theorem).  

**Nature punishes three. Three is the first number that allows cycles.**

## 2. Binary gives a clean decision boundary  
With two choices:  
- One is **A**, the other **not‑A**.  
- Feedback can be **negative** (correct toward A) or **positive** (reinforce A).  

With three choices (A, B, C):  
- “Not‑A” is ambiguous (could be B or C).  
- Any voting rule (majority, plurality) can produce **intransitivity** (A beats B, B beats C, C beats A).  

Binary avoids cycles. **Cycles are death to governance.**

## 3. Biological and cognitive roots  
Neurons fire or don’t fire (binary).  
Genes are expressed or repressed (binary).  
Survival decisions: **fight or flight**, **eat or not eat**, **mate or not mate**.  

Trinary would require a third option like “negotiate” — which is just a delayed binary.  

**Evolution selected binary because it’s fast, cheap, and error‑correctable.**
