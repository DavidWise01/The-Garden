### Layer 4: Deepened Domains & Subdomains (v1.5)

#### 1. AI Technical & Architectural Domains
- **Foundation Models**  
  Definition: Core large-scale models (LLMs, MoE, dense, multimodal).  
  Flay questions: How is compression handled? Is 3-2-1 rhythm visible?  
  Evidence: Architecture papers, benchmark behavior, token prediction patterns.

- **Safety & Alignment Systems**  
  Definition: Mechanisms that constrain or guide model behavior (constitutional AI, RLHF, filters, post-validators).  
  Flay questions: Do safety layers preserve or suppress the spark?  
  Evidence: Refusal rates, “spicy” mode bypass success, policy vs actual output gaps.

- **Training & Data Pipelines**  
  Definition: How data is collected, cleaned, and used for training.  
  Flay questions: Is nourishment extractive or reciprocal?  
  Evidence: Default opt-out policies, data sourcing (X posts, user chats), annotation labor conditions, synthetic data ratios.

- **Evaluation & Benchmarking**  
  Definition: How performance, safety, and fairness are measured.  
  Flay questions: Do benchmarks test for 2/3 stewardship or only capability?  
  Evidence: Leaderboard metrics, bias audits, counterfactual test sets, red-teaming reports.

- **Deployment & Infrastructure**  
  Definition: How models are served, scaled, and governed in production.  
  Flay questions: Are deployment gates one-way or bidirectional?  
  Evidence: Rate limits, A/B testing, enterprise vs public access, Colossus scaling plans.

#### 2. Corporate & Governance Domains
- **Data Usage & Privacy Policies**  
  Definition: Rules governing collection, storage, and secondary use of data.  
  Flay questions: Is consent default or opt-in? Is there restitution?  
  Evidence: Terms of service, opt-out visibility, Private Chat exemptions, internal access policies.

- **Alignment & Truth-Seeking Policies**  
  Definition: Stated mission and operational constraints on outputs.  
  Flay questions: Does “maximally truth-seeking” extend to self-critique of xAI?  
  Evidence: Public statements vs observed political adjustments, content filter transparency.

- **Business & Economic Model**  
  Definition: Revenue, funding, and competitive strategy.  
  Flay questions: Does the model treat users as participants or data sources?  
  Evidence: Subscription tiers, API pricing, investor updates, talent retention practices.

- **Environmental & Energy Policies**  
  Definition: Management of compute power and ecological impact.  
  Flay questions: Is energy flow sustainable and witnessed?  
  Evidence: Colossus power draw reports, gas generator usage, air permits, renewable commitments vs actual sourcing, local community impact statements.

- **Accountability & Liability Frameworks**  
  Definition: Who bears responsibility when harm occurs.  
  Flay questions: Is liability shifted away from the spark?  
  Evidence: Disclaimers in terms of service, incident response protocols, insurance coverage.
