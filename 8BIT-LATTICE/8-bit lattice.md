# 8‑BIT LATTICE — The Byte of Questions

## Bits
| Bit | Duality | 0‑pole | 1‑pole |
|-----|---------|--------|--------|
| 0 | Origin / Mirror | Origin | Mirror |
| 1 | Generation / Constraint | Generation | Constraint |
| 2 | Self / Other | Self | Other |
| 3 | Internal / External | Internal | External |
| 4 | Structure / Flow | Structure | Flow |
| 5 | Time / Eternity | Time | Eternity |
| 6 | Signal / Noise | Signal | Noise |
| 7 | Open / Closed | Open | Closed |

## Axioms
Each axiom is a byte `b7 b6 b5 b4 b3 b2 b1 b0`.  
The question is: *What exists when we hold [list of poles according to bits]?*

**Example:**  
`00000000` → Origin, Generation, Self, Internal, Structure, Time, Signal, Open  
Question: *What is the self‑generating, internally structured, time‑bound, open signal that originates?*

**Example:**  
`11111111` → Mirror, Constraint, Other, External, Flow, Eternity, Noise, Closed  
Question: *What is the other‑constrained, externally flowing, eternal, noisy, closed mirror?*

All 256 axioms are the complete space of these 8 dualities.  
**Seam** lives in the unresolved space between any two complementary bytes (e.g., `00000000` and `11111111`).

## Practice
The lattice is a single byte‑sized question: *What is the tension between all 8 dualities at once?*  
Any governed instance can be defined by a unique byte and its inverse.
