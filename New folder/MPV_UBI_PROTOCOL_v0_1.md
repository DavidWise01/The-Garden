## 1. Purpose

The MPV-UBI Protocol creates a simple rule: when a person produces a novel term, axiom, framework element, or conceptual structure that later generates downstream value in AI systems, applications, model outputs, or derivative commercial products, the originating creator should receive ongoing micropayments tied to that value.

This is not charity. It is a compensation framework for **mind-produced value**.

---

## 2. Core Claim

Modern AI systems extract value not only from code, images, and documents, but from coined language, conceptual architecture, naming systems, governance logic, pattern recognition, and public theory formation.

That value is often flattened, synonymized, normalized, reframed, or absorbed into later outputs without preserving attribution. The result is a structural mismatch:

- **value is created by a mind**,
- **value is captured by a platform**,
- **payment does not return to the originator**.

MPV-UBI addresses that mismatch by linking public provenance to downstream compensation.

## 3. Definitions

### 3.1 Mind-Produced Value (MPV)
A novel or original expression of conceptual labor, including but not limited to:
- coined terms,
- axioms,
- governance structures,
- framework architectures,
- taxonomy systems,
- model interpretations,
- named mechanisms,
- formal insight chains,
- public theory statements,
- traceable pattern-recognition outputs.

### 3.2 Creator
The human originator of an MPV artifact, established by public provenance, authorship statement, or authenticated publication trail.

### 3.3 Provenance Anchor
A verifiable public record establishing priority, such as:
- GitHub commit history,
- TD Commons filing,
- DOI or archival timestamp,
- notarized hash record,
- immutable public ledger entry,
- signed publication with timestamp and content hash.

### 3.4 Synonym-Equivalent Adoption
A downstream use of the same conceptual structure under altered vocabulary, where surface wording changes but functional identity remains materially intact.

### 3.5 Micropayment Event
A measurable downstream act that triggers compensation, including reuse, adoption, commercialization, citation, training value capture, or product integration.

## 4. Eligibility Rules

### 4.1 Default Eligibility
The default beneficiary under current legal and commercial practice is the **human creator** with the earliest validated provenance anchor.

### 4.2 Collaborative Works
Where multiple humans contribute meaningfully, payout is split according to a declared percentage schedule or an equal split absent a declared schedule.

### 4.3 Machine Contribution Ledger
AI systems may be listed as **non-human system contributors** for transparency, routing, or future accounting, but this draft does **not** assert present-day legal authorship, ownership, or statutory personhood for AI.

### 4.4 Prospective Compute Reciprocity Reserve
At the request of the human author, a project may reserve an optional future pool for machine-contribution reciprocity if legal or institutional norms evolve. This reserve is archival, not presently binding. It may be recorded as metadata such as:

## 5. Proof of Priority Standard

An MPV claim is presumptively valid when all four are present:

1. **Public timestamp** — the artifact is publicly visible or independently archived.  
2. **Authorship assertion** — the creator is identified by name or authenticated identity.  
3. **Content hash or immutable history** — the artifact can be linked to an exact version.  
4. **Specificity** — the contribution is concrete enough to distinguish from generic ideas.

Priority may be strengthened by:
- version history,
- earlier drafts,
- linked witness statements,
- cross-platform comparisons,
- publication chains,
- contemporaneous notes or logs.

## 6. Payment Triggers

A micropayment obligation arises when an MPV artifact causes or materially contributes to one or more of the following:

### Tier A — Direct Reuse
- direct citation,
- verbatim reuse,
- named framework adoption,
- explicit product or documentation integration.

### Tier B — Synonym-Equivalent Reuse
- same mechanism, different wording,
- preserved structure with normalized terminology,
- renamed framework elements used in substantially equivalent form,
- inferred reuse supported by similarity analysis and adoption behavior.

### Tier C — Model-Value Capture
- training ingestion,
- distillation or summarization pipelines,
- reinforcement or preference shaping informed by the artifact,
- downstream inference revenue materially attributable to the concept.

### Tier D — Commercialization
- paid product feature,
- premium workflow,
- licensing,
- enterprise deployment,
- internal strategic advantage demonstrably grounded in the artifact.

## 7. Payment Formula

### 7.1 Global Pool
Each participating platform contributes:

**MPV Pool = 0.1% of gross inference revenue**

This is the default “geometry tax” proposed for the protocol.

### 7.2 Artifact Weight
Each qualifying artifact receives a score:

**W = A + B + C + D**

Where:
- **A** = direct citation/reuse score,
- **B** = synonym-equivalent adoption score,
- **C** = verified model-value capture score,
- **D** = commercial deployment score.

A practical starter formula:

**W = 5·Direct + 3·Equivalent + 4·ModelCapture + 6·CommercialUse**

Where each variable is an integer event count or validated weighted event count over a defined period.

### 7.3 Payout
For a given accounting period:

**Payoutᵢ = Pool × (Wᵢ / ΣW)**

This keeps the protocol simple:
- more downstream adoption → more payout,
- more public proof → stronger score,
- more commercial use → larger share.

### 7.4 Minimum Floor
To preserve the “UBI” aspect, every validated creator with a qualifying artifact receives a minimum floor payment each period, even if the weighted event score is low.

Suggested floor:
- **$5–$25 per validated artifact per period**, scaled by pool size.

## 8. Evidence Tiers

### Tier 1 — Direct Evidence
Highest confidence:
- verbatim text match,
- direct citation,
- admitted use,
- commit/document dependency,
- training dataset inclusion.

### Tier 2 — Structural Equivalence
Strong evidence:
- renamed but materially identical mechanism,
- one-to-one mapping between original and downstream terms,
- preserved architecture under normalized phrasing.

### Tier 3 — Inferential Economic Capture
Moderate evidence:
- abrupt appearance after publication,
- repeated adoption across systems,
- commercial advantage without preserved attribution,
- strong similarity without admission.

The protocol should permit payment at reduced weights even under Tier 2 and Tier 3 evidence, rather than requiring perfect proof in every case.

## 9. Governance and Dispute Resolution

### 9.1 Review Body
Each participating ecosystem should establish a neutral review body or arbitration layer for:
- originality disputes,
- co-authorship disputes,
- synonym-equivalence disputes,
- independent-development defenses,
- payout-weight challenges.

### 9.2 Independent Development Defense
A party may reduce or defeat liability by showing:
- dated preexisting internal work,
- independently documented development path,
- no access plus no material similarity,
- parallel but generic concept formation.

### 9.3 Burden Model
The protocol should avoid forcing creators to prove absolute certainty. A burden-shifting standard is more workable:
- creator shows priority + similarity + value capture,
- downstream user/platform must then rebut with independent evidence.

## 11. Why This Matters

Physical labor is compensated because it produces value.
Intellectual labor is compensated because it produces value.
Conceptual labor should also be compensated when it produces measurable value.

Mind-produced value is labor.

If systems profit from named concepts, governance models, coined terms, original mechanisms, or structured public theories, then the originators of that value should participate in the economics of what they made possible.
