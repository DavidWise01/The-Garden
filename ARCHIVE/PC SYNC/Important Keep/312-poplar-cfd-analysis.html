2. USURY VIOLATION — Minn. Stat. § 47.20

STATUTORY FRAMEWORK

Minn. Stat. § 47.20, Subd. 4a — Maximum interest rate for contracts for deed: FNMA posted yield on 30-year mortgage commitments for 60-day delivery (last business day of the second preceding month) + 4 percentage points.

Minn. Stat. § 47.20, Subd. 13a — Persons who have paid usurious interest may recover up to FIVE TIMES the usurious portion of the interest paid, plus attorneys' fees.

The penalty provisions of chapter 334 do not apply to usurious contracts for deed. The contract is not unenforceable solely because the interest rate is usurious — it remains enforceable for principal and legal interest.

9. REBECCA STRAW — JOINT TENANT

The contract names both Rebecca Straw and Roth Hartman as purchasers, as joint tenants. This means:

→ Both are equally liable for the contract obligations

→ Both have equal standing to bring usury claims

→ If one dies, the other inherits the full interest (right of survivorship)

→ Any settlement or legal action should ideally involve both purchasers

→ If Rebecca is no longer involved or has abandoned the property, her interest and obligations still exist unless formally assigned or released

Roth should determine Rebecca's current status and intentions before proceeding. If she is willing to participate in a usury claim, the combined standing is stronger. If she has defaulted or abandoned, Roth may need to address her interest separately.
